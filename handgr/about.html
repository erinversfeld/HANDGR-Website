About Us

The project and the people.

HANDGR began when a young student with too many majors grew frustrated with the fact that she couldn't attend South African Sign Language lectures due to course collisions.

Exposure to new gesture recognition technologies left her with the question: why does this learning have to be constrained to the classroom? Why can't we take it home with us?

A reality check or two later, and HANDGR was born: a project which seeks to harness the power of commercial gesture recognition technologies for a tool to help to teach South African Sign Language to the hearing.
